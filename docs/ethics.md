## Ethical Considerations

## Risks
**Misdiagnosis** - Users may interpret the system’s predictions as professional medical advice and rely on them for health decisions. This could lead to delayed consultation with healthcare professionals or inappropriate self-diagnosis.
**Bias Across Skin Tones** - The model may perform better on certain skin tones if the training dataset contains more samples from those groups. This imbalance can lead to inaccurate predictions and unfair outcomes for underrepresented skin tone categories.
**Privacy / Data Security** - Skin lesion images may contain sensitive patient information that must be protected. Improper storage or handling of these images could lead to privacy violations or unauthorized access to personal medical data.

## Mitigations
**Include disclaimers** - The system will clearly state that it is intended only for educational or research purposes and should not replace professional medical advice. Users will be encouraged to consult qualified healthcare professionals for proper diagnosis and treatment.
**Dataset Selection** - The dataset will be curated to include images representing a wide range of skin tones and demographic groups. Model performance will also be evaluated across these groups to identify and correct potential biases.
**Data Security** - All images used in the project will be anonymized to remove any identifying information about patients. Data storage and processing will follow proper security practices and comply with relevant privacy regulations and ethical standards.
